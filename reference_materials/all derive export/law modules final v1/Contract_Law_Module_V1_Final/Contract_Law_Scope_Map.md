GEP Phase: 0.1 (Scope Identification)

Legal Branch: Contract Law (Ethiopian Civil Code)

Purpose: Define the twelve core modules (CL-01 to CL-12) covering the full lifecycle of a contract, from formation to termination and remedies.

ID Prefix

Concept/Module

Key Legal Focus (Civil Code Articles)

CL-01

Contract Formation (Offer & Acceptance)

Definition of Offer, Acceptance, Time, Place of Formation, Consent (Art. 1678-1698)

CL-02

Contract Validity (Formalities & Capacity)

Written Form, Authentication, Incapacity (Minors, Interdicts), Corporate Capacity (Art. 1709-1725)

CL-03

Defects of Consent (Vitiating Factors)

Mistake, Fraud, Duress (Coercion), Undue Influence (Art. 1699-1710)

CL-04

Contract Object (Possibility & Legality)

Object must be possible, lawful, and defined. Causa (Art. 1711-1718)

CL-05

Voidness and Voidability (Sanctions)

Absolute (Void) vs. Relative (Voidable) Nullity, Time limits, Effects of Nullity (Art. 1808-1818)

CL-06

Effects of Contract (Binding Force)

Pacta sunt servanda, Contract Interpretation, Relation to Third Parties (Art. 1731-1755)

CL-07

Performance of Contract (Execution)

Place, Time, Parties to Performance, Imputation of Payments (Art. 1756-1770)

CL-08

Non-Performance (Breach)

Definition of Default, Different Types of Non-Performance (Art. 1771-1775)

CL-09

Remedies for Non-Performance

Specific Performance, Pecuniary Damages (Indemnification), Reduction of Price (Art. 1776-1791)

CL-10

Termination of Contract (Dissolution)

Grounds for Termination (Ex: Mutual Consent, Non-Performance, Unforeseen Events) (Art. 1806-1818)

CL-11

Extinction of Obligations (General)

Payment, Novation, Compensation, Release (Art. 1819-1845)

CL-12

Extracontractual Liability (Torts)

Rules for Liability Arising from Damage (Fault-based and Strict Liability) (Art. 2028-2178)
